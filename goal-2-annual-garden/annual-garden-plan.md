# Annual Garden Plan — Full Nutritional Coverage for Two People

This plan covers all vegetables and crops that must be replanted each year (annuals and
short-lived biennials). Together with Goal 3 (perennial plan), this forms a complete
food system for one man and one woman.

Quantities are sized for two people at full self-sufficiency. Adjust down 20–30% if you
are supplementing with animal products (eggs, dairy, meat, fish).

---

## Preservation Strategy

Without preservation, a summer garden cannot feed two people year-round.

| Method            | Best For                                | Notes                                        |
|-------------------|-----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| **Root cellar**   | Potatoes, squash, carrots, beets, turnips | 32–40°F, high humidity; most calorie crops  |
| **Canning**       | Tomatoes, beans, fruit, pickles         | Pressure canning required for low-acid foods |
| **Drying**        | Beans, corn, seeds, herbs, peppers      | Easiest long-term storage                    |
| **Fermenting**    | Cabbage, cucumbers, beets, peppers      | Adds probiotics; extends season 6–12 months  |
| **Freezing**      | Leafy greens, peas, corn, broccoli      | Blanch first; requires power                 |

## Nutrient Gaps in This Annual Plan

These must be supplemented from the perennial plan (Goal 3) or animal products:

- **Vitamin B12** — not in any plant. Eggs from chickens or dairy cover this.
- **Vitamin D** — minimal; mushrooms (UV-dried) + sunlight.
- **Iodine** — use iodized salt or sea kelp flakes.
- **Selenium** — 1–2 Brazil nuts per person per day.
- **Complete omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** — flax/hemp provide ALA only; add eggs, fish, or rabbits.
