# Perennial Garden Plan — One-Time-Plant, Plant It and Forget It

Full nutritional coverage for one man and one woman using only perennial plants:
trees, bushes, shrubs, and other crops you plant once and harvest for years or decades.

No annual replanting. Every plant listed here regrows on its own each season.

Priority is given to **heirloom, legacy, and open-pollinated varieties** with the highest
known nutrient density. These varieties were selected before industrial breeding flattened
flavor and nutrition in favor of appearance and shelf life.

---

## Strategy Overview

A perennial garden alone cannot easily hit every nutrient target. Use this layered approach:

1. **Calorie base** — nut trees, root perennials, seed-bearing shrubs
2. **Protein base** — nitrogen-fixing legume trees/shrubs, high-protein perennials
3. **Micronutrient layer** — leafy perennial greens year-round
4. **Fruit layer** — vitamins C and antioxidants from berry bushes and fruit trees
5. **Fat layer** — nut trees and seed perennials
6. **Soil fertility** — nitrogen-fixing species throughout

For complete nutrition, pair with:
- **Chickens** (eggs cover B12, complete protein, EPA/DHA)
- **Iodized salt or kelp** (iodine gap)
- **1–2 Brazil nuts/person/day** (selenium)

## Space Planning

A mature perennial system for two people requires less space than annual gardening
because yields compound over time and no bed-prep is needed.

| Zone             | What Goes Here                                     | Space       |
|------------------|----------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| Zone 1 (close)   | Herbs, chives, sorrel, ramps, perennial kale       | ~200 sq ft  |
| Zone 2           | Asparagus, rhubarb, Jerusalem artichoke, horseradish | ~400 sq ft |
| Zone 3           | Berry bushes, currants, gooseberry, aronia, sea buckthorn | ~800 sq ft |
| Zone 4           | Fruit trees, hazelnuts, elderberry, pea shrub      | ~2,000 sq ft|
| Zone 5           | Nut trees (chestnut, walnut, hickory, pecan)       | ~5,000+ sq ft |
| **Total**        |                                                    | **~8,400+ sq ft (~0.2 acre for core; nut trees need 0.5–2 acres)** |

Nut trees take the most space but deliver the most calories per hour of labor once mature.
Two chestnut trees + two hazelnut bushes alone can provide a meaningful calorie contribution.

## Nutrient Gaps and How to Fill Them

Even a full perennial garden has a few gaps. These are the non-negotiable additions:

| Gap             | Solution                                                           |
|-----------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Vitamin B12     | 4–6 chickens (eggs daily); or dairy goat                           |
| Iodine          | Iodized salt at table; or 1 tsp dried kelp flakes/week             |
| Selenium        | 1–2 Brazil nuts per person per day (plant a few trees if zone 9+)  |
| EPA/DHA omega-3 | Chickens on pasture; or small fish pond (perch, tilapia)           |
| Vitamin D       | Sunlight; UV-exposed dried mushrooms; a few hens in sun            |
| Calories (gap year 1–3) | Annual potatoes/squash/beans while trees establish        |
