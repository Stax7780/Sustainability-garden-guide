Soil, Seeds & Animals

The three systems that close the loop — rotating crops so the soil stays healthy, saving seeds so you never buy again, and integrating animals to fill the nutritional gaps no garden can cover on its own.

Crop Rotation

Never plant the same family in the same bed two years in a row. Rotation prevents disease buildup, balances soil nutrients, and breaks pest cycles naturally. Follow this 4-year plan for every annual bed.

The 4-Year Rotation Cycle

YearFamilyCropsEffect on Soil
Year 1 Legumes Beans, peas, soybeans, peanuts Adds nitrogen — fixes N from air into soil for next crop
Year 2 Brassicas Kale, cabbage, broccoli, Brussels sprouts, cauliflower, turnips Uses nitrogen — hungry feeders that thrive on legume leftovers
Year 3 Roots & Alliums Carrots, beets, parsnips, onions, garlic, leeks Loosens soil — deep roots break compaction; alliums suppress soil pathogens
Year 4 Fruits & Nightshades Tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, squash, corn, potatoes Heavy feeders — amend with compost; follow with legumes next year
Key rule: Tomatoes, potatoes, peppers, and eggplant are all nightshades (Solanaceae). Never follow one with another — they share blight and nematode pressure. Allow at least 3 years between nightshade plantings in the same bed.

Rotation for Small Spaces — 3-Bed Minimum

If space is tight, compress to a 3-bed rotation: Legumes → Brassicas + Roots → Fruiting crops. Always move in one direction. Label beds A, B, C and rotate A→B→C→A each spring.

What Doesn't Rotate

CropWhy It Stays Put
AsparagusPerennial — dedicated permanent bed; don't disturb roots
RhubarbPerennial — permanent bed; improves with age
GarlicException: rotate every 3 years to prevent white rot buildup
StrawberriesRenew bed every 3–4 years; move to fresh ground to prevent disease
All perennial vegPermanent beds — build soil around them, don't rotate

Succession Planting

Stagger sowings so you harvest continuously instead of all at once. Without succession planting you get a glut in June and nothing in August.

Seed Saving

Save seeds from open-pollinated and heirloom varieties and you never buy seeds again. Hybrids (marked F1) don't breed true — always save from OP or heirloom stock.

Seed Storage Rules

RuleDetail
Cool + dry + darkIdeal: 35–50°F, below 50% humidity. A sealed jar in the refrigerator works perfectly.
Label everythingVariety name, year saved, location. Unlabelled seeds become useless in 2 seasons.
Test viabilityBefore planting old seeds: place 10 on a damp paper towel, fold, wait 7 days. If fewer than 6 sprout, buy fresh.
Silica gel packetsAdd one to each jar to absorb moisture. Recharge in 250°F oven for 30 min when saturated.
Save from best plantsAlways save from the healthiest, most productive plants — not the first or last to fruit.

Soil Building

Healthy soil grows healthy food. A self-sufficient system must feed the soil as much as it takes from it. The goal is living soil — teeming with fungi, bacteria, and worms that do the feeding work for you.

Compost System

InputTypeC:N RatioNotes
Kitchen scraps (veg only)Green (N)15:1No meat, dairy, or cooked food
Fresh grass clippingsGreen (N)20:1Don't pile thick — layer with browns
Fresh manure (chicken, rabbit)Green (N)10:1Hot-compost first; don't apply raw
Dried leavesBrown (C)60:1Shred for faster breakdown
Straw / hayBrown (C)80:1Excellent mulch base
Cardboard (unprinted)Brown (C)350:1Wet before layering; smothers weeds
Wood chipsBrown (C)400:1Surface mulch only — don't dig in
Comfrey leavesGreen (N)10:1Activator — accelerates whole pile

Target ratio: 3 parts brown : 1 part green. Keep moist (wrung-out sponge), turn every 2–4 weeks. Ready in 2–4 months when it smells like earth and you can't identify original materials.

Animal Integration

No garden fully covers all nutritional needs. Four to six chickens solve the three hardest gaps — B12, EPA/DHA omega-3, and complete protein — while producing the best fertilizer available and controlling pests.

The four nutrient gaps only animals reliably fill: Vitamin B12 · EPA/DHA omega-3 · Vitamin D (from pasture eggs) · Complete bioavailable protein. You can patch these with supplements — or you can keep four chickens.

Chickens — The Core Animal for This System

FactorDetail
Number needed4–6 hens for two people; provides 24–36 eggs/week at peak lay
Best breedsRhode Island Red, Plymouth Rock, Sussex — dual-purpose (eggs + meat), hardy, good layers
Space4 sq ft/bird inside coop; 10 sq ft/bird in run minimum. More is always better.
FeedLayer pellets + kitchen scraps + pasture. Pasture eggs have 3–6× more omega-3 than confined eggs.
Nutritional output per egg6g complete protein, 70 kcal, B12, D, selenium, choline — one egg covers a meaningful share of daily targets
Manure value~1 lb/bird/day; highest N of any common livestock manure — hot-compost before applying
Pest controlRotate through garden in fall after harvest — scratch up grubs, slugs, and overwintering pests
Garden integrationMove in mobile pen ("chicken tractor") across garden beds before planting each spring
