# Companion Planting — Symbiosis Guide

Plants that work together grow better than plants that work alone. This guide covers
beneficial relationships across both the annual garden (Goal 2) and perennial system (Goal 3).

---

## The Core Principle

Every plant in this system should serve at least two roles:
1. Feed the people
2. Feed the soil, repel pests, attract pollinators, or shelter a neighbor

---

## The Three Sisters (Annual Foundation Guild)

The oldest known companion planting system. Three plants that support each other completely.

| Plant       | Role                                                                 |
|-------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Corn**    | Vertical trellis for beans to climb; provides shade for squash      |
| **Beans**   | Fixes nitrogen from air into soil, feeding corn and squash          |
| **Squash**  | Broad leaves shade out weeds, retain soil moisture, deter pests     |

Plant in a mound. Corn first, then beans 2 weeks later, then squash. Minimum 3x3 ft per guild.
Scale up: 10x10 ft per guild for meaningful yield.

## Perennial Food Forest Guilds

A guild is a cluster of plants centered on one main crop (usually a tree), each playing a role.

### Apple / Pear Tree Guild

| Plant                    | Role                                              | Position        |
|--------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|-----------------|
| **Apple or Pear tree**   | Central crop                                      | Center          |
| **Comfrey**              | Deep taproot mines minerals, leaves are mulch     | Drip line       |
| **Chives / Garlic chives**| Repel apple scab and aphids                      | Under canopy    |
| **Nasturtium**           | Trap crop for aphids (draws them away from tree)  | Outer ring      |
| **White clover**         | Nitrogen fixing, feeds beneficial insects         | Ground cover    |
| **Yarrow**               | Attracts predatory wasps that eat caterpillars    | Outer ring      |
| **Daffodil**             | Repels deer, moles, voles from root zone          | Drip line ring  |

### Chestnut / Nut Tree Guild

| Plant                    | Role                                              | Position        |
|--------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|-----------------|
| **Chestnut or Walnut**   | Central crop                                      | Center          |
| **Siberian Pea Shrub**   | Nitrogen-fixing windbreak; edible seeds           | North/west side |
| **Comfrey**              | Mineral accumulator; chop-and-drop mulch          | Drip line       |
| **Ramps / Wild leek**    | Ground cover under canopy; shade tolerant         | Under canopy    |
| **Ostrich fern**         | Shade-tolerant ground cover; edible fiddleheads   | Under canopy    |
| **Black currant**        | Mid-layer fruit; thrives in partial shade         | Outer drip line |
| **White clover**         | Nitrogen, pollinator support                      | Ground cover    |

### Berry Shrub Guild (currants, gooseberry, aronia)

| Plant                    | Role                                              | Position        |
|--------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|-----------------|
| **Berry shrub**          | Central crop                                      | Center          |
| **Comfrey**              | Mulch, mineral mining                             | Beside shrub    |
| **Chives**               | Repel aphids and gooseberry sawfly                | Base of shrub   |
| **Nasturtium**           | Trap crop for aphids                              | Outer ring      |
| **Strawberry**           | Ground cover; fills gaps; vitamin C crop          | Ground cover    |
| **White clover**         | Nitrogen, weed suppression                        | Ground cover    |

## Annual Companion Pairings

### Beneficial Combinations

| Plant              | Good With                        | Why It Works                                                  |
|--------------------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Tomatoes**       | Basil, marigold, carrot, parsley | Basil repels aphids + whitefly; marigolds repel nematodes     |
| **Carrots**        | Chives, rosemary, leeks, onions  | Alliums repel carrot fly                                      |
| **Brassicas**      | Dill, celery, chamomile, sage    | Dill attracts wasps; sage repels cabbage moth                 |
| **Beans**          | Carrots, squash, marigold        | Beans fix nitrogen for neighbors                              |
| **Corn**           | Beans, squash, melon             | Three Sisters logic; beans feed corn                          |
| **Squash/Pumpkin** | Corn, beans, nasturtium          | Nasturtium repels squash beetles                              |
| **Peppers**        | Basil, carrot, marjoram          | Basil improves flavor and repels aphids                       |
| **Lettuce**        | Tall crops (tomatoes, corn)      | Needs afternoon shade in summer; uses space efficiently       |
| **Garlic**         | Roses, stone fruit, brassicas    | Repels aphids, Japanese beetles, borers                       |
| **Potatoes**       | Horseradish, marigold, beans     | Horseradish repels Colorado potato beetle                     |
| **Asparagus**      | Tomatoes, parsley, basil         | Tomatoes repel asparagus beetle; asparagus repels nematodes   |

### Combinations to Avoid

| Avoid Planting Together     | Reason                                                   |
|-----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Onions + beans/peas         | Alliums stunt legume growth                              |
| Fennel + almost everything  | Allelopathic; keep isolated or in a pot                  |
| Potatoes + tomatoes         | Same family; share blight disease                        |
| Walnut trees + most veg     | Juglone (allelopathic compound) toxic to many plants     |
| Brassicas + brassicas       | Same pest and disease pressure; rotate annually          |
| Corn + tomatoes             | Both heavy nitrogen feeders; compete aggressively        |

## Nitrogen Fixers — Feed the Whole System

These plants pull nitrogen from the air and deposit it in the soil. Plant them throughout.

| Plant                        | Type       | Where to Use                                    |
|------------------------------|------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| **White / Red Clover**       | Ground cover | Between all beds; orchard ground cover         |
| **Beans (dry and fresh)**    | Annual     | Rotate through every bed; follow with heavy feeders |
| **Peas**                     | Annual     | Early spring crop before main crops go in       |
| **Siberian Pea Shrub**       | Shrub      | Windbreak on north/west; fixes 70–100 lbs N/acre/yr |
| **Autumn Olive**             | Shrub/tree | Edge of property (check local invasive rules)   |
| **American Groundnut**       | Vine       | Trellis near heavy-feeding trees or beds        |
| **Honey Locust** (edible)    | Tree       | Canopy nitrogen fixer; pods are livestock fodder |

## Dynamic Accumulators — Mine Minerals from Deep Soil

These plants have deep taproots that pull up calcium, potassium, and trace minerals
from layers shallow-rooted plants cannot reach. Chop and drop as mulch.

| Plant          | Minerals Concentrated              | How to Use                                |
|----------------|-------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Comfrey**    | Calcium, potassium, phosphorus, B12 | Chop leaves 4–6x/season; use as mulch    |
| **Yarrow**     | Potassium, copper, phosphorus       | Let self-seed at bed edges                |
| **Chicory**    | Calcium, potassium                  | Grow in rough areas; chop before seeding  |
| **Dandelion**  | Calcium, iron, potassium            | Let grow in paths and orchard             |
| **Horsetail**  | Silica, calcium                     | Compost tea or dried mulch               |
| **Borage**     | Potassium, calcium                  | Annual that self-seeds; edible blue flowers |

## Pest Control Through Planting

### Repellent Plants

| Pest                      | Repellent Plants                              |
|---------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Aphids                    | Chives, garlic, marigold, nasturtium (trap)   |
| Carrot fly                | Rosemary, sage, chives, onions                |
| Cabbage moth / butterfly  | Sage, thyme, mint, dill                       |
| Colorado potato beetle    | Horseradish, tansy, marigold                  |
| Squash vine borer         | Nasturtium, blue hubbard squash (trap crop)   |
| Japanese beetle           | Garlic, chives, rue                           |
| Deer                      | Daffodil ring, lavender, sage, yarrow         |
| Voles / moles             | Daffodil bulbs as border ring around beds     |

### Beneficial Insect Attractors

| Beneficial Insect          | Plants That Attract It                         |
|----------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Honeybees                  | Borage, phacelia, clover, lavender, fruit bloom|
| Predatory wasps            | Yarrow, dill, fennel (keep isolated), carrot flower |
| Lacewings                  | Angelica, dill, coriander                      |
| Ground beetles             | Dense ground cover; mulch; clover paths        |
| Hoverflies                 | Phacelia, sweet alyssum, marigold              |
