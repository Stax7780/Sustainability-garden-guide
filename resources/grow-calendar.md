# Grow Calendar — Month by Month

For one man and one woman. Temperate climate (USDA Zones 5–7 baseline).
Adjust 2–4 weeks earlier for zones 8+, 2–4 weeks later for zones 3–4.

Key: SI = Start Indoors | DS = Direct Sow | TP = Transplant | H = Harvest | P = Plant/Establish | PR = Preserve/Store

## Monthly Task Overview

### January – February
- Review seed inventory; order seeds and bare-root stock
- Start peppers and eggplant indoors (late Feb)
- Prune dormant fruit trees and grape vines
- Check root cellar stores; use oldest first
- Plant garlic in mild zones

### March
- Start onions, leeks, kale, broccoli, cabbage indoors
- Direct sow spinach, arugula, peas, parsnips, flax outdoors (cold-tolerant)
- Harvest ramps and ostrich fern fiddleheads (early March, forest edge)
- Divide chives, sorrel, walking onions
- Apply compost to all perennial beds before growth begins

### April
- Transplant onions, kale, broccoli, cabbage outdoors
- Start tomatoes, peppers, squash, cucumbers, sweet potatoes indoors
- Direct sow beets, carrots, turnips, radishes, lettuce
- Plant potatoes
- Harvest asparagus begins; rhubarb stalks; sea kale shoots; sorrel
- Plant bare-root fruit trees and berry bushes (if not done in fall)

### May
- Transplant tomatoes, peppers, squash after last frost
- Direct sow beans, corn, zucchini, cucumbers, amaranth, quinoa
- Transplant sweet potatoes
- Asparagus harvest continues (6–8 weeks total)
- Serviceberry ripens (late May in zone 6)
- Cherry (sour) begins to ripen (late May–June)
- Plant any remaining perennial crowns (asparagus, rhubarb)

### June
- Full harvest of asparagus ends; let fern grow
- Harvest: rhubarb, chives, sorrel, good king henry, perennial kale, sea kale
- Harvest: strawberries, gooseberry, red currant, mulberry, cherry, apricot
- Begin harvesting zucchini, peas, lettuce, arugula
- Succession sow lettuce, spinach for fall crop
- Ferment first batches: sauerkraut from early cabbage

### July
- Peak summer harvest: tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, beans, zucchini
- Harvest: black currant, cornelian cherry, blueberry, raspberries, grapes begin
- Harvest garlic (when tops die back)
- Dry herbs, chilis, beans
- Preserve: can tomatoes, pickle cucumbers, dry peppers
- Begin drying flax and amaranth for seed harvest

### August
- Harvest: winter squash, sweet corn, pumpkins, dry beans
- Harvest: elderberry, sea buckthorn, aronia, goji, grapes, pears begin
- Harvest: hazelnut, apples begin, blueberry continues
- Preserve: elderberry syrup, sea buckthorn juice, can pears
- Plant fall brassicas: kale, chard, bok choy, broccoli, cabbage, spinach
- Start garlic from saved bulbs (plant October)

### September
- Harvest: chestnuts, walnuts, butternuts, more apples, late pears
- Harvest: Jerusalem artichoke, skirret, scorzonera, horseradish begin
- Harvest: hardy kiwi, rugosa rose hips, grapes (continue)
- Harvest fall root veg: beets, carrots, turnips, parsnips
- Fill root cellar: potatoes, squash, roots
- Plant garlic for next year
- Divide and replant rhubarb and asparagus crowns if needed

### October
- Final harvest: pecans, late apples, persimmons begin
- Bring in last sweet potatoes and winter squash
- Harvest rutabaga, leeks, Brussels sprouts (sweeter after frost)
- Root cellar fully stocked
- Plant spring bulbs (daffodil border rings for deer repelling)
- Apply thick mulch to all perennial beds before freeze
- Plant garlic (last chance in zones 5–6)

### November – December
- Harvest: persimmons (after frost), Brussels sprouts, leeks, kale (frost-sweetened)
- Jerusalem artichokes can be harvested through winter as needed
- Perennial kale harvestable in mild zones
- Review the year: what yielded, what failed, what to adjust
- Order seed catalogs; plan next year's layout
- Maintain root cellar; check for rot monthly
